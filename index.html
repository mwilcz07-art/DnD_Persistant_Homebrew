Hatred's Persistance

This particular individual has been shot, stabbed, clubbed, poisoned, and so much more. They have died more than any creature has any right to. Yet they persist, learn, but more importantly, come back. Not of necromancy, nor of divine or cursed intervention, but of sheer will and hatred. Their restless souls cannot leave a score, debt, or grudge unsettled. Some will even come back out of spite to death itself.

RULE I — TOTAL FLESH DESTRUCTION

If all flesh associated with the character’s soul is destroyed, the character dies permanently and cannot return.

This includes but is not limited to:

  • Disintegration effects
  • Total incineration to ash
  • Complete acid dissolution
  • Divine or planar annihilation
  • Any effect that leaves no viable material for reconstruction

If any flesh or compatible material remains, the soul may attempt to persist.

RULE II — RECONSTRUCTION THROUGH REFUSAL

When reduced to 0 HP and Rule I is not triggered: No death saving throws are made. The character is considered dead. After hours or days (DM discretion), the soul rebuilds a body using available materials.

Partial Reconstruction Without Death

If a body part is removed or destroyed without the character reaching 0 HP, the character may still undergo reconstruction to replace only that part, provided:

  • The loss was not intentional
  • Adequate time and materials are available
  • The process cannot occur during combat, a short rest, or any similarly immediate timeframe

The DM determines narrative feasibility and duration.

RULE III — WEAPON TOKENS

With each death, there's a lesson. This will determine whether that lesson was learned.

Gaining Weapon Tokens

Upon each death and successful reconstruction:

  • Gain 1d3 Weapon Tokens
  • The character may hold no more than 3 Weapon Tokens at any time
  • Excess tokens are lost

Spending Weapon Tokens

Weapon Tokens may only be spent during reconstruction to integrate weapons directly into the body.

Integrated weapons:

  • Are part of the body
  • Do not occupy hands
  • Cannot be disarmed or dropped
  • Use normal attack actions
  • Do not grant extra attacks

Weapon Token Costs

  • 1 Token: Simple weapon, light martial weapon, or natural weapon like claws, horns, and stingers
  • 2 Tokens: Heavy martial weapon or integrated ranged weapon
  • 3 Tokens: Specialized adaptive weapon designed to counter the most recent cause of death (DM approval required)

RULE IV — POST-MORTEM INTEGRITY CHECK

When the character is reduced to 0 HP and Rule I is not immediately triggered, determine how much of the body remains viable for reconstruction. This represents:

  • Overkill damage
  • Method of death
  • Environmental hazards
  • Violence after the killing blow

View Post-Mortem Integrity Table

Results

  • Success: That body part survives and may be reused
  • Failure: That body part is destroyed and must be replaced

If a body part has previously failed a Soul Claim, it automatically fails this check.

RULE V — PHYSICAL STAT OVERWRITE

When a body part is replaced, all previous physical modifiers from that part are discarded to make way for the new ones. Note: Your body is a loadout, not a progression tree. You do not evolve. You reconfigure.

RULE VI — VITALITY RECALCULATION

Whenever CON changes due to reconstructed body parts, HP is recalculated like so. 1) Subtract: (Old CON modifier × Character Level). 2) Add: (New CON modifier × Character Level)

Clarifications

  • Applies only to CON changes from body parts
  • Does not affect CON from class features, feats, spells, or items
  • Max HP cannot be reduced below 1 by this rule alone

RULE VII — SOUL CLAIM

When reconstructing with foreign flesh, the soul must assert ownership to remain anchored. This process is called a Soul Claim.

WHEN SOUL CLAIM ROLLS OCCUR

After reconstruction is complete, make one Soul Claim roll for each replaced body part:

  • Head
  • Torso
  • Arms: 1 roll per arm
  • Legs: 1 roll per leg

You may make 1 roll per part replaced.

THE SOUL CLAIM ROLL

For each replaced part, roll:

1d20 + CON modifier + proficiency

The DC is determined by the DM based on how alien or hostile the flesh is.

SUGGESTED DC GUIDELINES

  • DC 10 — Familiar Humanoid Flesh (Human, elf, dwarf, orc)
  • DC 12 — Monstrous Flesh (Troll, ogre, gnoll)
  • DC 13 — Beast Flesh (Bear, spider, hydra)
  • DC 14 — Undead Flesh (Zombie, revenant, dracolich)
  • DC 15 — Construct Components (Golem, clockwork)
  • DC 16 — Fiendish Flesh (Devil, demon)
  • DC 17 — Aberrant Flesh (Mind flayer, aboleth)
  • DC 18 — Celestial Flesh (Angelic beings)
  • DC 19–20 — Primordial or Divine Matter (Titan, god-shard)

The more wrong the flesh is, the harder it is to convince reality that it belongs to you.

OUTCOMES

SUCCESS

  • The soul claims the flesh
  • The body part fully integrates
  • The part anchors the soul
  • No further Soul Claim rolls are required unless the part is replaced again

FAILURE

  • The flesh remains foreign
  • The body part functions mechanically
  • The part does not anchor the soul
  • Mark the part as Unclaimed

Unclaimed parts:

  • Still grant their stat modifiers
  • Still count as physical matter

RULE VIII — CONTINUITY OF FLESH

Only body parts that fail the Post-Mortem Integrity Check are destroyed.

What Carries Over

  • Surviving body parts
  • Their stat modifiers
  • Natural armor
  • Claimed or unclaimed status

Surviving parts do not reroll, recalc stats, or require new Soul Claims.

All Weapon Token augmentations are always lost upon death.

RULE IX — MOMENTUM OF HATRED

Each death grants one Hatred Mark, escalating rage and instability.

Benefits

  • +1 bonus to Initiative per Hatred Mark
  • During the first round of combat, once per turn, add CON modifier to the damage of one successful attack

Costs

  • Long rests require +4 hours per Hatred Mark
  • At the end of each long rest, make a CON save: DC = 10 + Hatred Marks. Failure: gain 1 level of exhaustion.

Exhaustion gained this way cannot be removed except by completing a long rest.

Hatred Marks have no upper limit and are removed only by major narrative intervention. (In short, DM's discretion)

RULE X — MENTAL DEGRADATION

Each death increases Wisdom by +1 at the cost of -1 to either Intelligence or Charisma.

If you reduced CHA once already, and would like to reduce it again, you must succeed on a CON saving throw or reduce INT instead. The DC for this starts at 12 and increases by +3 for every successful throw until failure. Once you fail the DC becomes 12 again.

Terminal Thresholds

  • INT 3 or lower: The character becomes bestial and increasingly inhuman
  • INT 0: The soul loses all capacity for cognition, becomes inert, and the character permanently dies
